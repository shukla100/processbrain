Notes from internal sync — new business account pipeline

- Priya's team gets the signed application from the client first, plus their
  formation docs (EIN, articles of incorp). She kicks it over to Marcus's team
  once that's in.
- Marcus's team does the KYB stuff — beneficial ownership check, runs OFAC/sanctions
  screening through ComplyAdvantage. Most of this is automated through Alloy but
  flagged cases go to manual review.
- Anything flagged (crypto-adjacent businesses, MSBs, weird ownership structures)
  needs Marcus himself to sign off, not just the analyst on his team. This has been
  a bottleneck — sometimes sits for a week with nobody pinging the client.
- Once compliance clears it, Elena's team sets up the actual account in the ledger
  and links it to the partner bank. She mentioned this can't happen before compliance
  signs off, ever — hard rule, not just a guideline.
- After the account's live, Sam's team issues API keys if the client wants
  programmatic access (a lot of our startup clients do, since they're integrating
  with their own finance stack).
- Jordan's team does the actual "welcome" handoff and becomes the ongoing point of
  contact. Right now this happens kind of informally — sometimes Jordan finds out
  the account is live from Slack, not a system trigger.
- Nobody really owns what happens after onboarding — like helping the client set up
  actual treasury/cash sweep stuff. That seems to fall through the cracks right now.
